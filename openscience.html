Open Science

Sharing code, knowledge and data openly are fundamental to the progress of science. In that spirit, I am involved in the development of many open source toolboxes. I am a core contributor to MNE-Python, a software package for MEG and EEG data analysis.
I also co-developed the Brain Imaging Data Structure (BIDS) for MEG. It's a standard to enable easy data sharing between researchers. The MNE-BIDS
package is a toolbox I maintain that helps achieve this goal. I also maintain pyglmnet, a Python package to fit regularized general linear models. [Blog post] by my younger self reflecting on the reproducibility crisis in the context of neuroimaging.
